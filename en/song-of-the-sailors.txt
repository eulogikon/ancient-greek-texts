Song of the Sailors, Roman era Koine poet of the 2nd-3rd century CE. Two surviving nautical poems. Free to read and download from the Greek library archive.

Nauton Kantionkyle, meaning "Song of the Sailors," is a title rather than a personal name. No biographical information exists for the poet designated by this name. Standard scholarly references and digital libraries contain no record of an author by this title.

The author is associated with two works, but their titles and content are unattested in extant ancient literature or the consulted sources.

If genuine, these poems could represent a form of popular nautical song from the second or third century CE, potentially offering insight into the language and culture of Eastern Mediterranean sailors. However, without any surviving fragments or reliable mentions, their historical and literary significance cannot be assessed.
