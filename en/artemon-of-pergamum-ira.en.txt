Artemon of Pergamum, a Hellenistic historian of the 2nd century BCE. Historical fragments in later sources. Free to download from the Greek library archive.

Artemon of Pergamum was a Hellenistic historian active in the 2nd century BCE. He lived in Pergamum, a major cultural center under the Attalid dynasty renowned for its library. No details of his personal life survive.

His only known work is On the Dedications at Delphi. The text is lost, preserved only in fragments cited by later authors like Athenaeus in his Deipnosophistae. Artemon’s significance lies in these fragments, which were used by later compilers as a source on offerings at the Delphic sanctuary. They provide valuable glimpses into the cultural and religious history of Delphi, representing the antiquarian tradition of cataloguing notable objects at Panhellenic sites.
