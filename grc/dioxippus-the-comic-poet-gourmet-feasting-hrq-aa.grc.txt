Fragments on Gourmet Feasting is a collection of eleven surviving excerpts from a lost comedy by the Athenian playwright Dioxippus. The work's title, which can also be rendered as Fragments Concerning a Dainty Feast, centers on the concept of luxurious and greedy consumption. Preserved through quotations in later ancient authors, most notably the gastronomic writer Athenaeus, the fragments were later compiled by modern scholars of Greek comic poetry.

The extant lines depict conversations filled with elaborate culinary terminology, showcasing a preoccupation with specific, often luxurious foods and dining paraphernalia. Characters discuss items such as womb, tripe, and special Rhodian cups, while mentions of hanging meats and coolers point to the detailed, sometimes absurd, portrayal of banquet preparation and gluttonous desire. This content aligns with known conventions of Old Comedy, using food and feasting as vehicles for social satire. The fragments likely critiqued excessive living, the behaviors at aristocratic symposia, and the familiar comic type of the parasite who lives for free meals.

Though the full scope and plot of Dioxippus's original play cannot be reconstructed, these fragments remain significant for illustrating the thematic breadth of Athenian comedy beyond its major figures. They provide valuable evidence for how 5th-century BCE comedy treated daily life, material culture, and social manners, using the universal subject of food as a source of humor and cultural critique.
