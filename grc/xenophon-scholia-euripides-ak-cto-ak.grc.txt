Scholia-Euripides
Σχόλια εἰς Εὐριπίδην
by Xenophon Scholia

Period   Classical
Dialect  Mixed Dialects
Domain   Grammar
Format   Verse

The Scholia on Euripides is a corpus of ancient grammatical and explanatory annotations preserved in the margins of medieval manuscripts containing the plays of Euripides. These notes represent a layered compilation of scholarship from various periods, primarily the Byzantine era, and serve as a critical resource for textual criticism, lexicography, and literary interpretation. The scholia address a wide range of subjects, including variant readings, explanations of rare vocabulary, clarifications of mythological and historical allusions, and observations on meter and dramatic technique. Their transmission is uneven across the Euripidean corpus, with some plays possessing extensive commentary and others very little. While a grammarian named Xenophon is recorded in the Suda lexicon as an author of commentaries, a specific, discrete collection of Euripidean scholia attributed to him is not substantiated by the major manuscript traditions or standard scholarly references. Regardless of precise authorship, the Euripidean scholia as a whole are an indispensable tool for modern editors and critics, preserving valuable fragments of lost ancient scholarship and illuminating the history of the text's reception and interpretation from antiquity through the Middle Ages.
